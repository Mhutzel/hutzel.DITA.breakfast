Avocado Toast

Avocado toast is so trendy right now and allows you to come up with new twists and variations on the original.

This recipe requires a skillet and a knife. You will also need the following ingredients:
  • 6 oz baby Portobello mushrooms, washed and sliced
  • 1 1/2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 avocado
    Note: Peel back the stem of the avocado to check for ripeness. If it comes away easily and is green underneath it is ripe and ready to eat.
  • 1 lime, halved
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • chili flakes to taste (optional)
  • 2 slices of bread
  • Prep time: 10 minutes
  • Cook time: 15 minutes
  • Serves: 1

Directions:

  1. Add the oil to a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add in the sliced mushrooms and sauté for 8-12 minutes or until golden brown, stirring occasionally but not too often
  2. Slice open the avocado
    1. Grip the avocado gently on one side with one hand. With a sharp knife, cut the avocado lengthwise around the pit and twist to split the avocado in half
    2. Gently tap the pit with a knife with enough force so that the knife edge wedges into the pit, but not so hard as to cut all the way through it
    3. With the edge of the knife, twist the pit out of the avocado and discard
      Note: This method is tricky and you must be careful so you don't cut your hand. For safety, fold up a kitchen towel and use that to hold the avocado half with the pit
  3. Scoop out the flesh of the avocado and add it to a bowl along with the juice of one lime
  4. Use a fork to mash the avocado and season with salt, pepper and chili flakes (optional)
  5. Toast the slices of bread
    Note: Check out this list of breadsto be sure that you're buying vegan.
  6. To assemble, slather the slices of toast with mashed avocado and top with sauteed mushrooms
  7. Serve immediately
